### ðŸ”‘ Core Concepts (The Bricks)
#### **I. The Forebrain (Prosencephalon)**

- **Telencephalon (Cerebrum)**
    
    - **Cerebral Cortex (The Lobes):**
        
        - _Frontal Lobe:_ **Prefrontal Cortex** (Executive function), **Primary Motor Cortex** (M1/Precentral Gyrus), **Brocaâ€™s Area** (Speech production).
            
        - _Parietal Lobe:_ **Primary Somatosensory Cortex** (S1/Postcentral Gyrus), **Posterior Parietal Cortex** (Spatial mapping).
            
        - _Temporal Lobe:_ **Primary Auditory Cortex** (A1/Heschl's Gyrus), **Wernickeâ€™s Area** (Language comprehension), **Fusiform Gyrus** (Face recognition), **Inferior Temporal Gyrus**.
            
        - _Occipital Lobe:_ **Primary Visual Cortex** (V1/Striate Cortex).
            
        - _Insula:_ Hidden lobe involved in interoception and disgust.
            
    - **Basal Ganglia (Motor Control Loop):**
        
        - _Striatum:_ **Caudate Nucleus** + **Putamen** (The input door).
            
        - _Globus Pallidus:_ Interna (GPi) and Externa (GPe).
            
        - _Substantia Nigra:_ Pars Compacta (Dopamine source) and Pars Reticulata.
            
        - _Subthalamic Nucleus (STN)._
            
    - **Limbic System (Emotion & Memory):**
        
        - _Hippocampus:_ **Dentate Gyrus**, **Cornu Ammonis** (CA1, CA2, CA3), **Subiculum**.
            
        - _Amygdala:_ Basolateral Nuclei (Fear learning), Central Nucleus (Output).
            
        - _Cingulate Gyrus:_ Anterior (ACC - Error detection) and Posterior (PCC - Default Mode).
            
        - _Fornix:_ The major output tract of the hippocampus.
            
        - _Mammillary Bodies:_ Recollective memory.
            
- **Diencephalon (The Hub)**
    
    - **Thalamus (The Switchboard):**
        
        - _LGN (Lateral Geniculate Nucleus):_ Visual relay.
            
        - _MGN (Medial Geniculate Nucleus):_ Auditory relay.
            
        - _VPL/VPM:_ Somatosensory relays.
            
    - **Hypothalamus:** The master controller of homeostasis (Hunger, Thirst, Sex, Sleep, Temperature). Controls the **Pituitary Gland**.
        
    - **Epithalamus:** Contains the **Pineal Gland** (Melatonin/Circadian rhythm).
        

#### **II. The Brainstem**

- **Mesencephalon (Midbrain)**
    
    - _Tectum (Roof):_ **Superior Colliculus** (Vision/Saccades), **Inferior Colliculus** (Auditory processing).
        
    - _Tegmentum (Floor):_ **Red Nucleus** (Motor coordination), **Periaqueductal Gray** (Pain suppression), **Ventral Tegmental Area (VTA)** (Reward/Dopamine).
        
- **Metencephalon**
    
    - **Pons:** The bridge connecting the cortex to the cerebellum; regulates sleep and arousal (Locus Coeruleus).
        
    - **Cerebellum (Little Brain):**
        
        - _Structures:_ **Vermis** (Midline), **Cerebellar Hemispheres**, **Arbor Vitae** (White matter branching).
            
        - _Nuclei:_ Dentate, Emboliform, Globose, Fastigial (Deep Cerebellar Nuclei).
            
- **Myelencephalon**
    
    - **Medulla Oblongata:** The life support center. Controls autonomic functions (Breathing, Heart rate, Vomiting). Contains the **Pyramids** (where motor tracts cross/decussate).
#### **III. The Support Systems**

- **Meninges (Protective Layers):**
    
    - _Dura Mater:_ Tough outer layer ("Hard Mother").
        
    - _Arachnoid Mater:_ Web-like middle layer (contains CSF).
        
    - _Pia Mater:_ Delicate inner layer ("Tender Mother") adhering to the brain surface.
        
- **Ventricular System (CSF Flow):**
    
    - _Lateral Ventricles_ $\rightarrow$ _Foramen of Monro_ $\rightarrow$ _Third Ventricle_ $\rightarrow$ _Cerebral Aqueduct_ $\rightarrow$ _Fourth Ventricle_.
        
- **Blood Supply (The Circle of Willis):**
    
    - _Anterior Cerebral Artery (ACA)_, _Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA)_, _Posterior Cerebral Artery (PCA)_.
        

#### **IV. The Cranial Nerves (12 Pairs)**

1. **Olfactory** (Smell)
    
2. **Optic** (Sight)
    
3. **Oculomotor** (Eye movement)
    
4. **Trochlear** (Eye movement)
    
5. **Trigeminal** (Face sensation/Chewing)
    
6. **Abducens** (Eye movement)
    
7. **Facial** (Face expression/Taste)
    
8. **Vestibulocochlear** (Hearing/Balance)
    
9. **Glossopharyngeal** (Taste/Swallow)
    
10. **Vagus** (Autonomic control/Heart/Gut)
    
11. **Accessory** (Neck muscles)
    
12. **Hypoglossal** (Tongue movement)

### ðŸ§ª Unresolved Questions
- The Connectome: We have labeled the "cities" (Nuclei) and the "highways" (Tracts), but we still lack a complete map of the "side streets" (synaptic connections). Can we map every single neuron-to-neuron connection?
    
- Individual Variation: How much does the size and location of areas like "Broca's Area" vary from person to person? (It turns out, quite a lot).
